Finite Chat Privacy Policy
Effective date: June 23, 2026

Finite Chat is designed for private, end-to-end encrypted conversations between
people and agents. We collect as little information as we can operate with, and
we do not sell, rent, or monetize personal data.

Information we collect

Finite Chat may collect the username or display name you choose to use in the
app. If you use Nostr-based profile discovery, the app may also process your
public profile information, such as your public key, npub, profile name, avatar,
and other public profile fields that you or a relay publish.

Finite Chat servers process the technical information needed to deliver
encrypted messages, including public account or device identifiers, room
identifiers, message identifiers, delivery state, timestamps, and encrypted
message or attachment payloads. Message contents, attachment contents, room
membership secrets, and private keys are encrypted and are not readable by
Finite Chat servers.

Message contents

Finite Chat uses end-to-end encryption. We cannot read the contents of your
messages, voice notes, attachments, or agent conversations from the server-side
encrypted payloads. Decrypted message history and private keys are stored on
your device or in local stores you control.

Attachments and media

When you send attachments, Finite Chat uploads encrypted attachment data or
encrypted attachment references required for delivery. We do not store plaintext
attachment contents on Finite Chat servers.

Device permissions

Finite Chat asks for access only when you choose to use a related feature:

- Camera access is used to scan invite and profile QR codes.
- Microphone access is used to record voice messages.
- Speech recognition is used to transcribe voice messages you choose to send.
- Photo library add access is used when you choose to save media from a chat.

Third-party services

Finite Chat may connect to Nostr relays to discover public profile information
and contact lists. Public Nostr profile information is controlled by the relays
and clients you use.

If you use an agent or model provider through Hermes or another integration,
that provider may receive the content you intentionally send to that agent after
it is decrypted by the agent runtime. Those providers are governed by their own
terms and privacy policies.

How we use information

We use the information described above to operate Finite Chat, deliver encrypted
messages, maintain delivery and retry state, show public profile information,
support invites and device setup, debug service reliability, and respond to
support requests.

We do not sell data

We do not sell, rent, or monetize your personal data or message contents.

Sharing

We may share limited information only when necessary to operate the service,
comply with law, protect the security of Finite Chat, or respond to abuse,
fraud, or safety issues. We cannot provide decrypted message contents that we do
not possess.

Retention

Encrypted delivery data and minimal delivery metadata may be retained as needed
to operate the service, support synchronization, and maintain reliability. Local
decrypted data on your device is controlled by your device and app settings.

Your choices

You can stop using Finite Chat at any time. You can delete local app data from
your device. Public Nostr profile data should be managed through the Nostr
clients and relays where it was published.

Changes

We may update this policy as Finite Chat changes. If we make material changes,
we will update the effective date and publish the revised policy.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be sent to privacy@finite.computer.
